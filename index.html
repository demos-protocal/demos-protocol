The problem

Two broken systems.
One solution.

Democracy runs on election cycles. Markets run on capital accumulation. Neither consistently serves ordinary people. Demos collapses both into a single act.

01

Broken democracy

We vote every few years and hope our representatives carry our will into institutions built to resist change. Wealth buys access. Access shapes policy. The loop closes.

02

Broken markets

Markets allocate resources efficiently — and indifferently. They don't ask what a community needs. They ask what returns a profit. Ordinary citizens are priced out at every level.

03

Separated power

Voting and spending have been separated from each other and captured by those with resources to game both. The ordinary citizen's vote comes once every few years.

→

The Demos answer

When you allocate tokens to a program, you simultaneously fund it and vote for it. The aggregate of all allocations, updated in real time, is the living budget of the community.

How it works

Three mechanics.
One system.

01 — Universal Entry

Every Oregon resident gets one free token

No purchase required. No technical knowledge required. A simple web registration links an Oregon address to a digital wallet and issues your Demos Token automatically. Everyone begins equal.

02 — Wealth Ceiling

No wallet can hoard beyond the cap

The ceiling is enforced at the protocol level — in code, not in policy. A transaction that would push a wallet above the limit is automatically rejected. Hoarding becomes structurally impossible.

03 — Investment = Vote

Your allocation is your ballot

Direct tokens to schools, veteran housing, local farms, clean energy, or small business grants. Each allocation simultaneously funds the program and registers your democratic preference. No election cycle. No interpretation.

"The people, and only the people, have the right to govern themselves — not through representatives who speak for them, but through systems that express their will directly."

Community investment pools

Where your tokens flow.

Citizens vote on which programs to fund. Returns flow back to the community commons — and get redeployed democratically. Wealth circulates. It doesn't accumulate.

→ Oregon Farm Succession Fund Buys farmland from retiring farmers via community land trust. Keeps Oregon agriculture in Oregon hands — not corporate acquisition. 4–7% est.
→ Local Business Microloan Fund Low-interest loans to Oregon small businesses. Repaid with interest back to the commons pool for democratic redeployment. 5–9% est.
→ Community Solar Cooperative Investment in Oregon clean energy projects with ongoing revenue share flowing back to the community. 6–10% est.
→ Affordable Housing Trust Develops workforce housing at below-market rents. Returns flow to the commons. Housing as a community asset, not an extraction tool. 3–6% est.
→ Veterans Enterprise Fund Priority loans to veteran-owned Oregon businesses. Community-backed capital for those who served Oregon. 5–8% est.
→ Immigrant Worker Cooperative Funds worker-owned cooperatives in agriculture and services. The community decides who participates — not the legislature. 4–7% est.
